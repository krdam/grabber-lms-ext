5. Case Study 2: Technology внедрениеInfusion in a Printing System

The printing industry is яростноfiercely competitive as many companies compete for market share. Currently, the trend in this industry is that the total number of pages printed in black and white are снижениеdeclining, while the total number of pages printed in color are increasing rapidly. Additionally, digital printing systems have begun competing with traditional смещенияoffset printing systems.

Companies compete to gain market share and profit by delivering increased customer value along several dimensions such as price, printer productivity improvements, service cost reduction, workflow improvement, and image quality improvements.

In a production printing system (a system where the print produced is the actual product sold to the end customer), all of these attributes are important. As a result, many innovative technologies are being developed that drive improvements in one or more of these attributes.

The technology внедрениеinfusion analysis (TIA) methodology was used to evaluate the величинаmagnitude of change propagation, cost, and benefits for this particular technology.

The first step is to characterize the current product by constructing the DSM representation of the system.

Before this can be done, the system needs to be decomposed into components and/or subsystems.

It's important to recognize that the scope and зернистостьgranularity of the DSM has an effect on the rest of the analysis and the subsequent ΔDSM.

The image below shows the complete DSM representation of the baseline printing system. The DSM consists of 84 elements and shows physical connections (black), mass flows (red), energy flows (green), and information flows (blue) within the system.

It took about 140 hours to construct this DSM representation of the baseline printing system. It required working with the engineers and product team to ensure that the DSM was correct.

In the image below, we can see the system within iGen3:

In this case, a предварительныйpreliminary demonstration of technology capability showed that a new approach using so-called auto-плотностьdensity correction was potentially жизнеспособныйviable and could address the defined need. The approach was selected, but the details of how to best implement the technology and an assessment of the общийoverall воздействиеimpact were still needed. The technology considered in this case study is one that усиливатьenhances the value of the next generation product by improving one of the following FOMs: the variety of media that can be printed, print speed, reliability, run cost, and image quality.

In step 2 of the process, the need for technology внедрениеinfusion has been identified. A representation of concept внедрениеinfusion into the baseline product can be constructed in the form of a ΔDSM.

To construct the ΔDSM:

1. Empty all cells of the baseline DSM.

2. To the baseline DSM, add new rows and columns for newly added elements (N1-N2) and insert the names of the new elements.

3. For newly added, removed, or modified elements and connections, fill in the соответствующийcorresponding cells of the ΔDSM using the color coding scheme.

4. Note that changes directly required by the new technology as well as indirect (propagated) changes should be included in the ΔDSM (Eckert et al., 2004; Griffin et al., 2007).

Below is ΔDSM for newly infused auto-плотностьdensity correction technology:

In this image, only those elements that are affected by the technology внедрениеinfusion are shown. общийOverall, there are 87 changes: 15 elements (components) that were either added, eliminated, or revised; 33 physical connection changes; no mass flow changes; 7 energy flow changes; and 32 information flow changes.

Using the number of connections and elements in the baseline DSM and in the ΔDSM, the TIE is calculated.

As it turns out, the внедрениеinfusion of the 34 image-correction technologies results in an 8.5% change to the original baseline system.

With the results of the ΔDSM, an estimate of the total engineering усилиеeffort in terms of time and resources for technology внедрениеinfusion was obtained. The technology внедрениеinfusion усилиеeffort falls into the following three categories:

  • Component design/redesign усилиеeffort
  • Interface design/redesign усилиеeffort
  • System integration усилиеeffort (including testing and validation)

In this case, with the introduction of a new technology into the system, a new performance model had to be developed that would predict the customer-perceived output performance, J, based on the 35 engineering variables available to the engineering and technology teams. This model supplemented and was correlated to laboratory test results in order to make the necessary performance predictions with confidence.

Cost models that evaluated both the expected change in the unit manufacturing cost of the общийoverall system and the expected change in the cost of producing prints with the new printing system were developed. These were primarily based on similar information collected for the existing printing system (iGen3) into which the new technology is potentially being injected.

The cost of producing prints is influenced by many factors, including, for example, the cost of materials to make prints and the cost of servicing the printing system.


Once the technical information for technology внедрениеinfusion has been gathered, the current product value in the market segment it is competing in needs to be estimated.

The new technology improves the customer-relevant system performance, thus increasing the number of units sold, and in this case, it also decreases the service cost to the company by further reductions in printing system downtime, labor, and parts.

The following general assumptions are made for revenue and cost воздействиеimpact calculations:

1. The new product will be produced for five years.

2. The operational service life of the product is five years.

3. воздействиеImpact on the revenue is realized by service cost reductions (less maintenance required) per every 1,000 prints.

4. There is a nonrecurring investment cost for three years before the launch of the product due to new technology внедрениеinfusion (R&D costs to develop and certify the technology and product).

5. There is added per-unit cost for the technology installed in individual products.

Nonrecurring investment cost, unit cost for the new technology module, and service cost savings per 1,000 prints were provided by the engineering team. A cash flow chart was then created.

Conclusion

The technology внедрениеinfusion framework was demonstrated черезthrough a printing system case study where a value-enhancing image correction technology was infused into an existing product to improve the performance of the system.

A baseline product DSM of dimensions N=84 x 84 and a technology ΔDSM were created to estimate the change propagation of the system and the actual усилиеeffort required to make the required changes. The DSM had a non-zero fraction of 3.7%, and the ΔDSM suggests a technology инвазивностьinvasiveness index of 8.5%. Performance improvement, revenue, and cost воздействиеimpact were estimated черезthrough expert engineering assessment and product attribute value curves. Finally, a range of possible financial outcomes was captured черезthrough the Monte Carlo simulation.

It was demonstrated that this methodology can be successfully implemented with reasonably available data. The total усилиеeffort to construct the baseline DSM model of the system was about 140–160 hours, while the entire technology внедрениеinfusion study took about six to nine months to conduct.
